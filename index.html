SANATAN DHARMA

Sanātana Dharma is an endonym used by Hindus to refer to Hinduism. It refers to the “eternal” truth and teachings of Hinduism. It can also be translated as “the natural and eternal way to live". The term is used in Indian languages alongside the more common Hindu Dharma for Hinduism.

Principal

Different texts give different lists of the duties, but in general sanatana dharma consists of virtues such as honesty, refraining from injuring living beings, purity, goodwill, mercy, patience, forbearance, self-restraint, generosity, and asceticism.

Importance

In Sanatana Dharma, dharma is crucial to personal growth and self-realization. By discovering your dharma and consistently striving to fulfill it, you can experience a sense of purpose and direction in life and live in a way that is true to yourself and in alignment with your deepest values and beliefs.
